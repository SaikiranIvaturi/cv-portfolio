## Background

An AI-assisted internal tool gives clinical analysts a way to model how protocol changes ripple through cost projections across a patient population. Model outputs are _distributions_, not point estimates — the UI had to communicate uncertainty clearly without overwhelming users.

## My role

I joined roughly two months after the initial build. The core architecture and Redux state model were already in place — my job was to extend the product and improve what was there.

- Built the **Research Recommendations** panel — surfacing protocol suggestions contextual to the active scenario
- Built the **Ideation Chat** interface — a freeform AI chat embedded alongside the modelling workflow
- Completely **redesigned Contract Search** — replaced the original flow with a cleaner search-first UX, reducing friction finding and comparing contracts
- Enhanced visual design and component styles across the product for consistency and polish

## Redesigning Contract Search

The original Contract Search was functional but slow to use — analysts had to step through a multi-screen flow to find and compare contracts. I redesigned it as a search-first surface: a persistent search bar, inline filtering, and comparison directly in the results view. The structure of the feature didn't change, but the way you got to what you needed did.

---

## Outcome

Live with internal analysts. The Ideation Chat and Research Recommendations gave analysts a way to interrogate the model in plain language alongside the data — something the original product didn't have. Contract Search feedback was noticeably better after the redesign.
